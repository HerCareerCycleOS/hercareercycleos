FAQ

Common Questions.

01 Is this just for women who track their periods? +
No. Her Career Cycle OS is built around understanding your biological phases, whether you track or not. The science applies to every woman with a menstrual cycle regardless of how much attention she has paid to it until now.
02 I have an irregular cycle. Can I still use this? +
Yes. The framework is designed to be flexible. You learn to read your energy and cognitive patterns as they shift, not to follow a fixed 28-day calendar.
03 Will my employer or colleagues need to know I am doing this? +
No. This is a private performance strategy. Everything you apply stays entirely within your own planning and decision making.
04 How is this different from general productivity advice? +
General productivity advice was built around a 24-hour hormonal cycle. Yours runs on 28 days. This system is built specifically for the biology you actually have.
05 I am on hormonal contraception. Does this still apply to me? +
Hormonal contraception changes your cycle but does not eliminate all hormonal variation. Some elements of the framework still apply. The free guide covers this in more detail.
About

The boardroom was not designed for your biology.
Your strategy should be.

Her Career Cycle OS was built on one simple observation: the tools women use to navigate corporate life were all designed without them in mind.

Performance reviews, meeting schedules, presentation slots, deadlines. All built around a 24 hour hormonal cycle. The one men have.

Women operate on a 28 day infradian rhythm. Four distinct phases, each with its own cognitive strengths, energy patterns, and decision making profile. Ignoring that cycle does not make it go away. It just means leaving performance on the table.

Her Career Cycle OS translates the science of female biology into practical career strategy. When to negotiate. When to present. When to lead the room and when to do your deepest thinking.

Not as a wellness practice. As a competitive advantage.

This is not about working less. It is about working with your biology instead of against it.
